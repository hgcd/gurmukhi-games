Data-backed Learning Results

Our initial pilot demonstrated that Saanjh’s digital platform significantly boosted student engagement and learning outcomes, particularly among those who had previously struggled with the language through traditional methods. Saanjh’s initial pilot was in Punjabi, with aspirations to expand to similar South Asian languages.

Students showed strong mastery of foundational letters, with 20 of the 42 characters recognized at over 80% accuracy, and retention remained high across commonly used letters. Games that reinforced sequencing and repetition were especially popular, while even more advanced activities like audio spelling and letter recognition kept students challenged and motivated.

These results highlight how Saanjh makes language learning both accessible and effective, helping children build skills and confidence step by step.

Battle-tested & effective

The pilot program with preschoolers (ages 5 and under) demonstrated measurable gains in early Punjabi language proficiency through Saanjh’s physical learning kits. In their very first year of formal testing, students achieved a class average of 77% on the final exam, with five students scoring above 95%. These results are especially significant given the challenges of building early literacy in heritage languages outside immersion environments, showing that young learners can successfully adopt foundational skills such as letter recognition, vocabulary, and sounds through consistent, engaging practice.
